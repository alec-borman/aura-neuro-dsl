# Case Study: Building AURA (Accelerated Ultradian Rhythmic Audio)

He wrote down a rule for himself: **No overplayed songs.** No "Texas Sun." No "Mo City Don." No hits that carry psychic weight. Only deep cuts, B-sides, instrumentals, and weird stuff.

Then he asked an LLM to help him sequence it.

## The Method (What He Actually Did)

He fed the LLM his Spotify history. The LLM didn't "know" his brain chemistry, but it knew the acoustic data of every track—tempo, key, energy, instrumentalness. It mapped those onto three simple variables he made up:

- **Prediction Error (Pe):** How much does this song surprise your brain? Polyrhythms = high. Four-on-the-floor = low.
- **Kinetic Salience (Ks):** How much does this song make you want to move? Sharp snares = high. Ambient pads = low.
- **Acoustic Safety (As):** How much does this song tell your nervous system "you are safe"? Warm analog = high. Harsh distortion = low.

Then he gave the LLM a prompt: *"It's raining. I'm coding. Don't let me burn out."*

The LLM output a playlist. Not random. Not algorithmic "for you" garbage. A sequence where the first hour was low Ks, high As (rainy, cozy). The second hour spiked Pe (complex jazz-fusion drums). The third hour dropped into vocal R&B (resilience for job hunting). The final hour ramped Ks and crashed As (aggressive rap for walking out the door).

He queued it manually on Spotify. 50+ tracks. Hit play at 3:00 PM.

## The Result

He coded for 90 minutes straight. No caffeine. No nicotine. No breaks.

He applied to jobs without spiraling.

He made ramen. Ate it. Felt cold and hollow after—exactly what should happen when a dopamine sprint ends. That's not failure. That's the trough.

He took a hot bath. Used magnesium cream. Let his nervous system reset.

At 5:45 PM, the playlist switched to Run The Jewels and Denzel Curry. He walked out the door, drove to Freddy's, and sat down with his dad fully present, not thinking about code or rejection emails.

The case study is this: **A broke, unemployed systems architect with no lab, no funding, and no degree in neuroscience reverse-engineered his own attention using nothing but Spotify and a text prompt.**

## Why It Matters

The commercial music industry optimizes for *time on app*. Not focus. Not recovery. Not transition. Not your actual life.

AURA is a different deal. It's a language anyone can use. You write a short script. You feed it to any half-decent LLM. You get back a playlist that is guaranteed to be a great time until you leave for dinner.

No algorithm owns your ears. No hit song hijacks your hippocampus. You are the compiler.
